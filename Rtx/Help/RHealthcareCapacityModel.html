Healthcare Capacity Model

Class Name

  • RHealthcareCapacityModel

Location in Objects Pane

  • Models > Model > Infrastructure > Healthcare > Healthcare Capacity

Model Description

Model Form

    \[{{\Gamma }_{abs}} + {{D}_N}=\left\{ \begin{matrix} {{\Gamma }_{t}}\cdot \left( 1-{{d}_{\text{pwr}}}\cdot \left( 1-P \right) \right)\cdot \left( 1-{{d}_{\text{wtr}}}\cdot \left( 1-W \right) \right) & ; & {{\eta }_{S}}=0 \\ \left\{ \begin{matrix} {{\Gamma }_{t}}\cdot \frac{r}{2} & ; & \text{before restoraion} \\ {{\Gamma }_{t}}\cdot r & ; & \text{before repair} \\ {{\Gamma }_{t}}\cdot \left( 1-{{d}_{\text{pwr}}}\cdot \left( 1-P \right) \right)\cdot \left( 1-{{d}_{\text{wtr}}}\cdot \left( 1-W \right) \right) & ; & \text{after repair} \\ \end{matrix} \right. & ; & {{\eta }_{S}}>0 \\ \end{matrix} \right.\] Important Notes:
  • The "potential capacity" response is the same as "absolute capacity" response with the difference that it is ascending during the recovery period.
  • The "available capacity" response is the same as "potential capacity" response with the difference that the post-hazard hospitalized injured people are subtracted from the potential capacity.
  • If it is desired that the capacity of the healthcare unit will be ascending during the recovery period, the "potential capacity" response must be used.
  • To consider the events of injuries treatment during the post-hazard period in Discrete Event Analyzer, the "available capacity" response must be used.

DDM sensitivities

  • No

Properties

Object Name

  • Name of the object in Rt
  • Allowable characters are upper-case and lower-case letters, numbers, and underscore (“_”).
  • The name is unique and case-sensitive.

Display Output

  • Determines whether the model is allowed to print messages to the Output Pane.

Healthcare Information Model

  • The associate Healthcare information model

Normal Demand

  • \({D_N}\) = The hospitalization demand on the healthcare unit in normal condition

Structural Damage Ratio

  • \({\eta _S}\) = Structural Damage Ratio

Nonstructural Drift Damage Ratio

  • \({\eta_{ND}}\) = Non-structural drift damage ratio

Nonstructural Acceleration Damage Ratio

  • \({\eta_{NA}}\) = Non-structural drift damage ratio

Content Damage Ratio

  • \({\eta_C}\) = Content damage ratio

Repair Status

  • \({\varsigma_{rep}}\) = Status of Repair operation of healthcare building (1 if completed, 0 otherwise)

Restoration Status

  • \({\varsigma_{rst}}\) = Status of Restoration (1 if completed, 0 otherwise)

Recapture Factor

  • \({r}\) = recapture factor accounts for the fact that the business-related losses from earthquakes can be recouped to some extent by working overtime after the event (FEMA NIBS 2012)

Power Serviceability

  • \({P}\) = The serviceability status of the upstream electricity provider

Power Dependency

  • \({d_{pwr}}\) = The dependency of the Healthcare unit on power

Water Serviceability

  • \({W}\) = The serviceability status of the upstream water provider

Water Dependency

  • \({d_{wtr}}\) = The dependency of the Healthcare unit on Water

Output

  • \({R_{abs}}\) = The absolute revenue of the Healthcare unit in terms of number of beds
    • The output is an automatically generated generic response object, which takes the object name of the model plus “AbsoluteResponse”.
    Note: The absolute capacity demonstrates the current capacity of the commercial unit based on its current condition.
  • \({R_{pot}}\) = The potential capacity of the Healthcare unit in terms of number of beds
    • The output is an automatically generated generic response object, which takes the object name of the model plus “PotentialResponse”.
    Note: The potential capacity features the condition that the capacity is ascending during the recovery period.
  • \({R}\) = The available capacity of the Healthcare unit in terms of number of beds
    • The output is an automatically generated generic response object, which takes the object name of the model plus “AvailableResponse”.
    Note: The available capacity features the condition that the post-hazard injured people are hospitalized and therefore, the serviceable capacity is reduced.
